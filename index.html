Paper Airplane Challenge

Day 1: Research & Brainstorm

Today's Objectives (TEKS §130.364)

  • Define the problem's criteria and constraints. (c)(1)(A)
  • Research and understand the scientific principles (aerodynamics) behind the problem. (c)(1)(D)
  • Brainstorm and sketch multiple possible solutions. (c)(1)(E)

Concept 1: The Design Process - "Ask & Brainstorm"

Every engineering project starts with questions. We need to understand the problem, the rules, and the goal. This is the "Ask & Identify" phase. Once we understand the challenge, we move to "Imagine & Brainstorm", where we come up with as many creative ideas as possible without judgment. A good engineer explores multiple paths before choosing one.

Concept 2: Aerodynamics - The Four Forces of Flight

For an airplane to fly, four forces must be in balance:
Lift: The upward force created by air moving over the wings.
Weight: The downward force of gravity pulling the plane down.
Thrust: The forward force that pushes the plane through the air (from your throw!).
Drag: The backward force of air resistance that slows the plane down.

Paper Airplane Challenge

Day 2: Build & Observe

Today's Objectives (TEKS §130.364)

  • Create physical prototypes based on your sketches. (c)(1)(F)
  • Collect qualitative data through observation. (c)(1)(G)

Concept 1: The Design Process - "Create"

This is the "Create" phase, where we build our first working models, called prototypes. The goal of a prototype isn't to be perfect; it's to learn from. By building three different designs, we can quickly see which ideas work well and which ones don't.

Concept 2: Aerodynamics - Stability

A good plane is a stable plane. Stability is affected by the Center of Gravity (the balance point of the plane's weight) and the Center of Pressure (the balance point of the lift from the wings). If the center of gravity is too far back, the plane will be unstable and stall. If it's too far forward, it will nosedive.

Concept 3: Tutorial - How to Fold a Classic Dart

If you're not sure where to start, here's how to fold a simple, reliable plane. You can use this as one of your three prototypes to have a good baseline for comparison.

1. Fold in half lengthwise.

2. Fold top corners to the center.

3. Fold the new top edges to the center.

4. Fold the wings down.

Knowledge Check!

Paper Airplane Challenge

Day 3: Analyze & Iterate

Today's Objectives (TEKS §130.364)

  • Analyze test results to identify flaws and opportunities for improvement. (c)(1)(H)
  • Modify or redesign a solution based on test data (Iterate). (c)(1)(I)
  • Collect quantitative data using appropriate tools (e.g., measuring tape). (c)(2)(C)

Concept 1: The Design Process - "Test, Evaluate, & Improve"

This is the heart of engineering! "Test & Evaluate" means we collect data—not just opinions, but real numbers. "Improve" (or Iterate) means we use that data to make one small, smart change to our design. By changing only one thing at a time, we can know for sure if our change helped or hurt performance.

Concept 2: Aerodynamics - Wing Shape

The shape of the wings is critical. Long, narrow wings (like a glider) are great for long, slow flights because they generate a lot of lift. Short, wide wings (like a jet fighter) are better for high speed and stability. The angle of the wings also matters. Wings angled slightly up (dihedral) add stability, while wings angled down (anhedral) can make a plane more maneuverable.

Day 4: Finalize & Reflect

Today's Objectives (TEKS §130.364)

  • Create a final, optimized product based on all collected data. (c)(1)(I)
  • Communicate the results and reflect on the design process. (c)(1)(J)

Concept 1: The Design Process - "Finalize & Communicate"

The final steps are to create your best possible product and then "Communicate" your results. Summarize your data, explain your choices, and reflect on what you learned.

Concept 2: Combining Design Elements

Great innovation often comes from combining existing ideas. Maybe Prototype #1 had very stable wings, but Prototype #3 had a sleek, fast nose. For your final design (Version 3), don't be afraid to create a hybrid!
